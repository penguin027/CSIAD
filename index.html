Abstract

We introduce CSIAD, the first method to detect logical inconsistencies in document images by leveraging cross-sample comparisons and large language models. Unlike traditional image fraud detectors that rely on visual traces, our method identifies anomalies through factual mismatches in semantically similar images. We further propose a fact-driven verification module that guides LLM reasoning with objective evidence, effectively reducing hallucinations.

Method Overview

Our pipeline consists of: (1) multi-modal retrieval to collect relevant images; (2) LLM-based rule generation; (3) fact-driven rule verification; (4) explanation-aware anomaly detection.
